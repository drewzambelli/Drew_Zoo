Polar Bear

Ursus maritimus

Giraffe Cat Camel

Polar bears (Ursus maritimus) are the largest land carnivores, known for their distinctive white fur and powerful build. Native to the Arctic region, they thrive on the sea ice that covers the Arctic Ocean, and are perfectly adapted to the cold environment.

These majestic animals primarily feed on seals, relying on their excellent swimming abilities and sharp sense of smell to hunt across vast frozen landscapes. Despite their size—males can weigh up to 1,500 pounds—polar bears are graceful swimmers, capable of covering long distances in the water.

Polar bears play a crucial role in the Arctic ecosystem, but their survival is threatened by climate change and the loss of sea ice. They are classified as vulnerable, with conservation efforts focused on protecting their natural habitat and securing their future in the wild.
