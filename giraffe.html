Giraffe

Giraffa Camelopardalis

Camel Cat Polar Bear

Giraffes (Giraffa camelopardalis), famous for their long necks, are the tallest land animals. Native to the savannas and open woodlands of Africa, their height gives them a distinct advantage in reaching leaves and vegetation high in the trees.

Despite their size — males can grow up to 18 feet tall — giraffes are graceful and can run at speeds of up to 35 miles per hour over short distances. Their long necks also aid in thermoregulation, helping them stay cool in the hot African climate.

Giraffes play a critical role in maintaining their ecosystem by pruning trees and promoting plant growth. However, habitat loss and poaching have led to a decline in giraffe populations, and conservation efforts are underway to protect these gentle giants and preserve their natural habitats.
