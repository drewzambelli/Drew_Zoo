Camel

Camelus dromedarius

Giraffe Cat Polar Bear

Camels (Camelus dromedarius) are are known as the "ships of the desert," distinguished by their long legs and unique humps. Native to arid regions, they are adapted to harsh environments, and capable of withstanding extreme temperatures and going for days without water.

These incredible creatures primarily graze on dry grasses and shrubs, using their strong teeth to navigate tough vegetation. Despite their large size—males can weigh over 1,300 pounds—camels are nimble and can travel vast distances, making them vital to many cultures for transportation and trade.

Camels play an essential role in desert ecosystems, supporting both local economies and traditional ways of life. However, habitat loss and climate change pose significant challenges to their future, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to ensure their continued presence in the wild.
